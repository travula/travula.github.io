1 Objective

The objective of this activity is to ensure overall web-app development skills of students. This is a team project which ensures coherence between students and teaches them the importance of team work.

2 Instructions

  • Please find list of all projects here (link). Please look at all the project details and submit your top-6 choices in this form (link).
  • All these projects are to be done in teams. Choose your team wisely. Expected team size is mentioned with each project.
  • Once you decide your team, please fill the form. Form link will open on [2017-03-24 Thu 19:55] and close at [2017-03-24 Thu 23:55]. Allocation of projects will be done on First-Come-First-Serve basis. If none of your selected projects is available, there will be a second round for the same. Each project will be allotted to maximum 2 teams.
  • If one student's name is found in 2 teams, the one filled earlier will be taken into consideration. Only one member per team has to fill the form.
  • You can also suggest your own project by filling this form (link). If you are filling this form, you should not fill the form above. Ideal team size should be 3-5 students.
  • TA mentors for each project have been mentioned with the project names. If you suggest your own project, TA mentors will be allotted based on availability. TA mentors may change after project allocation.
  • TAs will only mentor your project. Do not ping them to debug your code.

3 Project Instructions

  • You can use any language/framework/technology to build your web-app. You will be graded on the basis of your code's design, narration of your code and the features you implement.
  • All development work should be done on gitlab only. One of the students can make a repo and add other teammates and your TA mentor.
  • All kinds of plagiarism is strictly prohibited and will be penalised heavily.

4 Submission Instructions

  • All the projects will be manually evaluated by the mentors. You need to host your project on 127.0.0.1:5000. All projects should be on https only.
  • You need to submit a readme file with your source code which contains details about what your project does and how to use it. It should be a step-by-step guide to enable any third party to use your app.
