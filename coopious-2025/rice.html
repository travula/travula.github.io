4. Description of varieties

4.1. General

Both the varieties are grown in 2024 khariff season. No fertilizer, pesticide or herbicide is used. The land is tilled with a tractor, nursery beds were raised and later transplanted. Cover crops like sun hemp were raised and tilled into the land to provide fertility. Fermented cow urine was given every week to act as a starter for replenishing micro organisms. Harvesting was done with human labor since the stalk of moligolukulu grows up to 6 feet and the tillering is profuse making it difficult for a machine to harvest.

The way forward is to switch to natural farming to circumvent dependency on fossil fuel for tilling. Tilling also exposes the soil to sun resulting in the escape of carbon from soil to atmosphere contributing to greenhouse gases. Carbon has also another important role in conserving the moisture of the soil. The more the carbon in the soil the greater the moisture allowing earthworms and other micro organisms to flourish to create a richer ecosystem. Fukuoka has showed that yields in natural farming are comparable or higher than conventional agriculture using chemicals.

The price is set higher than the prices of hybrid varieties available in any super market since the yield is lower and the production costs are higher due to the greater extant of manual labor in our process.

We are working on a self organizing platform where it should be possible for all the stakeholders in a supply chain to democratically define semantics of any process in the system, evaluate the performance and push the process in its direction - equal compensation for equal labor time (if this is the definition of the system), non-hierarchical, sustainability etc. - through tighter feedback cycles. Therefore all the variables that are inherent to any production are visible to allow consumers make conscious decisions and to some extent remedy the ills that arise from commodity fetishism.

4.2. Moligolukulu

Moligolukulu is a long duration heirloom variety. The crop duration is 210-220 days. All heirloom long duration varieties are high in nutrition. Only the husk is removed while keeping the entire bran. This particular variety is rich in oil content and therefore tastes like cream. Any whole rice when cooked splits at the end but not this one. It is easy on the stomach and I relished chewing even cold rice several hours after rice is cooked. There are a very few farmers who are reviving this variety.

The best way to cook is to boil the rice with lot of water (may be four times the rice) without a lid. After the rice is 70% cooked, switch off the flame and let it rest for 5-10 minutes with lid on top of the bowl. The heat already present will cook the rice. After 5 minutes or little later the rice should be fully cooked. At this point, drain the water into a bowl and cook the rice without lid for another 6 - 8 minutes on a low flame to ensure extra moisture has evaporated. Adjust the timing accordingly. Switch off the flame and let the rice sit for another 10 minutes without the lid. Do not throw away the drained water, have it next day in the morning or any time when low on energy. It is a great probiotic.

4.3. Mysore Malliga

This is another medium duration (130-140 days) heirloom fine variety. The literal translation of the name of this variety is mysore jasmines. It is semi polished. This is a great substitute for any of the current popular hydrid fine varieties. The rice comes out really beautiful and is considered good for the children according to the internet mythology. Since it is organically grown and is a heirloom variety the default benefits are already present.
